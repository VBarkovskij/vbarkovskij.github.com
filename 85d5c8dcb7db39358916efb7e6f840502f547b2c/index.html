UX/UI Case study for Vodafone CRM Siebel system. Designing a better customer relationship experience.

The project is protected by a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA). So, I cant dive into all numbers and visually and freely demonstrate it.

Overview

Multinational telecommunication conglomerate that offers services worldwide. Over the past decade, digital technologies have transformed our lives. Wherever we are, technology plays a vital role in almost everything we do – and work is no exception.

Position in a Market

Today, we expect to communicate with anyone andaccess information and services from anywhere at any time. The key metrics of that area are speed and efficiency of given service. Just to be conquerable and provide high-end services you need to evolve, digitally. Provide a more fresh and high-level end-to-end solution packed with great service.

Technology

With Vodafone Siebel CRM system you can effectively manage your customers and field teams and increase your efficiency. With Vodafone CRM's document management, sales automation, lead data management, calendar, and activity tracking, marketing tools, inventory management, and purchasing modules, you can accelerate your interaction with prospective clients and provide full follow-up on the management and guidance of your sales teams.

Problem definition

Siebel CRM system was way to old to perform and be used by Agents. The time per task and general efficiency level was quite low. The interaction between logical sections was not coherent. As an addition, visually impaired users really struggle to use the software therefore we received problems for users not classified as visually impaired. Headaches and eyestrain are common. The Siebel UI upgrade has failed to cater to agents with disabilities. It is not compliant with the ‘Equality Act 2010’ and Vodafone could face penalties.

Goals & Objectives

Design and deliver a new CRM Siebel UI for Vodafone UK. In parallel to incarnate new business prepositions. As a sub-goal is to increase the loyalty of people with disabilities and special needs. We were using a Hiser Element Usability Matrix. We capture issues during research and analysis.

Business objectives
- Improve customer satisfaction by improving the call time.
- Increase in trading for segment.
- Help to increase sale on online conversion.
- Strengthen relationships and encourage ongoing dialogue.

Success metrics & benchmarking
- Increase an average customer interaction efficiency.
- Decrease Home Broadband, Pay Monthly, Sim-only sales transaction down.
- Drop time to competency in retail stores.
- Increase trading among high-value customers.

UX goals
- Make the agent interaction more coherent and sequential.
- Validate and improve accessibility issues.
- Create design architecture proposals informed by a research.
- Conducting interviews with management and other stakeholders.
- Test validation proposals with agents in their working environment.
- Reviewing and validation corporate communication such as websites, annual reports, and sales kick-off presentations.

Seeing the problems first hand

We spoke to agents about their user experience with Halo and Enabled during our site visit to the Stoke Contact Centre. We saw the problems first hand and recorded what we discovered. This helped us to develop a problem statement and set our objectives.

Introduction

User research testing and interview at the executive stage of the design process will explore in more details the target audience’s digital behavior in general, as well as their current digital behavior in relation to the Vodafone, Halo customer relationship management system redesign. We will also seek to explore how the target audience responds to the overall look, feel, content and site architecture and whether they will use the system preferably better than the old existing one, uncovering specific tools and section and prioritization behind that. The deliverables from the project will include a detailed report of the findings, together with recommendations.

Test Objectives

The key objectives of user testing have been identified as follows:
- Gain an understanding of the target audience; their backgrounds, barriers, needs, goals, frustrations, and motivations in terms of retail online usage.
- Gain an understanding of how the target audience will interact with the system (when, where, how, tasks, problems, barriers)
- Address what users want from the system, including what would encourage them to use the site more efficiently.



- Gather empirical evidence (qualitative and quantitative) about how well users complete tasks using real retail system environment.
- Capture opinions around the design, structure, content, language, messages, and tools
- Identify and describe any usability issues encountered.
- Propose practical solutions and recommendations for improvement.
- Capture opinions around those disciplines: Customer relations, Converged / Customer Care, Customer Saves, Customer Upgrades, New Acquisitions, Small Business.

Methodology

We are going to conduct on a one-to-one interview format with between 16 users representing the target audience. Each interview session will last approximately 30 minutes and will be recorded in a written form. As an addition to the interview we’re going to shadow the day-to-day tasks performed by real agents.

Pre-test interview

Prior to testing, we will interview the target audience to assess their current digital behavior in general, as well as their digital behavior in relation to the Vodafone, Halo customer relationship management system redesign. This will help create a more rounded picture of test participants involvement. We'll also seek to gain an understanding of their needs, characteristics, motivations, and frustrations, including what they would like to be added in order to increase the efficiency decide.

Task-based Usability Testing

During testing, participants will be asked to perform day-to-day tasks with real customers.

This helps gauge if agents can use the features and functions of the new system successfully and uncover detailed opinions around the design, content, and IA, as well as to capture any problem areas or usability issues encountered. We will explore how the agents cope with completing tasks, capturing success rates, in finding information and performing actions, ratings of satisfaction.

Post-test Survey

After the task-based usability exercise is complete we will conclude with an emotional response questionnaire to capture views about the look, feel and structure of the system and also uncover any final thoughts and ratings they can provide on their experience of the that. This will help identify and clarify likes, dislikes and any difficulties encountered and will uncover whether users find the Halo redesign valuable and better than the old system.

Report of Findings

The deliverable includes a report to highlight our findings, together with a number of recommendations for improvement. The final report will also verbal commentary from participants where appropriate.

What we achieved

We’ve completely turned off the Siebel screens and our 10,000 contact centre agents and 3,000 retail staff are all only using our new user interface. As we’ve moved through the different stages of pilot and testing, as it’s making a real difference to our people and to our customers. It’s easier to use, faster to navigate and simpler to master. The feedback we’ve had has been great and our agents are finding it a much better system all round.

“Time to competency” drop to just 4 weeks in our retail stores, when the old Siebel would take an extra 8 weeks longer.

-An average customer interaction is down from 20 screens to just 4.
-A Home Broadband sales transaction down from 43 minutes to 22 minutes.
-A Pay monthly sales transaction down from 28 minutes to 22 minutes.
-A Sim-Only upgrade from 20 minutes down to 14 minutes.
-Time to competency has reduced by 65%
-The time it takes to complete a Home Broadband sales transaction is down nearly 50%.
-The time it takes to complete a Pay monthly sales transaction down nearly 20%.
-The time it takes to complete a Sim-Only upgrade is down nearly 30%
-Average number screens an agent utilises, for a consumer interaction is down 75%

Overview

When we intend to travel from work to home or any different location, we won’t get the logistic simply ever. It is always desirable to have an easy affordable mode to drive.


Market & problem definition

The number of people traveling in the car keeps on rising. The Automotive industry has been always growing intensely. Global sales of passenger cars are forecast to hit 81.5 million vehicles in 2018 and it’s 11% (9 millions) of car increase comparing to the 2015. Concurrence thought the companies and branches are really tight and self-demanding. Just to be conquerable and to claim a good position in the market you need to provide a better customer experience as good as to keep fresh and up to date into the digital world. The application we’re working on was outdated due to a myriad of reasons. Along with the competition evolution and internal problems, the following factors are the reasons for the difficulties. So our task was to set the train back on track and create an app that will correspond to the existing market needs.

We were limited on time and budget. Usability testing were conducted without any support and full-blown lab provided. Methodology and Benefits

A new concept of Modularity is proposed for the app in order to fulfil the requirement of adaptability for possible future merge of other ŠKODA apps. On this proposal we opted to use the concept of Modularity. By definition Modularity is the use of small units, modules, which are created and designed independently, and afterwards assembled into systems. This concept allows the creation of various different systems using the same modules.



Benefits

User can easily access controls and key info about service on each screen level. Each service might contain several modules and many customization options. In case of adding new service, it fits in modular system without impact on overall structure of the app. This structure allows to add as many services and sections as necessary. One service might have several entry points. From each level of the app as well as from different sections, e.g. Map/Locations section and Garage/Vehicle section.



Information Architecture

Based on different inputs mentioned in analysis phase we decided to restructure the whole information architecture concept of the app. Our main goal was to group services of the app in a more intuitive and logical way. We rearranged the settings into Main Settings (related for the entire app) and service related settings. Now they appear in relevant context which improves usability. One of the key findings from usability testing was that different types of users searched functionalities in different ways. This inspired us to create several entry points for each service (explained in section Modularity).



Improvements of UI design, Wireframes

On hand-drawn wireframes we sketch optimised User Flows and reformulate previous version problems in new UI patterns. These improve functionality, hierarchy of the content, some logics, accessibility and behaviour of features. Also we keep in mind that each screen should be as simple as possible, to make it understandable.


Design concept achievements

As part of the design we also aligned all the branding, creating a new look and feel for the app that reflected the SKODA’s brand, but was completely new to the client. Luckily they loved it! The final stage was consumer testing. And again, we were positively surprised. Regardless of age or technical ability, everyone could complete simple operations. Now, less than one year after its launch, SKODA Connect app. has doubled the usage.
